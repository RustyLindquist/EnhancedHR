# Commercial Insight

## Human Augmentation > Human Automation

Most orgs look at AI primarily as an opportunity to automate human activity, expecting that increased organizational velocity at reduced cost will ultimately lead to sustainable competitive advantage.

It won’t.

The barrier is just so low that the most it can offer is a short-term competitive moat. AI automation is now a **commodity. It’s simply** 21st Century table stakes.

Worse still, executive sentiment seems to dwell on the cost-saving outcome of automation, as much as the speed gains it promises. Simply put, it’s an opportunity to do more, faster, with fewer people. 

This too is a false summit, and ultimately leaves you barren of your greatest resource: your people.

Durable competitive advantage will not be found in either acceleration alone, or people elimination, but rather through people augmentation. It’s found in getting good at recognizing how you can augment and enhance the value of our people by intelligently aligning and amplifying their work with AI.

But your people always have been (and always will be) your organization’s primary origin of long-term competitive advantage, because they are ultimately the only truly and lastingly novel thing you have. They are the daily decision makers of what work gets done, and how that work gets done.

This leads to an important distinction today, in understanding how organizational velocity leads to long-term competitive advantage.

## Organizational Velocity

To more deeply understand this we have to re-examine the root idea of velocity.

Velocity has two components: speed and direction.

When everyone is operating at the speed of AI, the real competitive advantage shifts to the second, and far more critical component of velocity — direction.

What is the direction toward which you are moving? What is your individual and organizational innovation trajectory?

This is true as each individual role, as much as it is for the overarching organization. Employees, teams and departments need to be independently considering not only how they can leverage AI to increase the speed at which they get work done (the new default expectation), but how they can rapidly pivot in the value they provide, and how they provide that value.

For so long, competitive advantage was achieved by doing more, faster, and with fewer resources. AI enables that. Not just for you, but for everyone.

### Decision Velocity

**organizational decision velocity**—how fast your people can find the right truth, interpret it correctly, and apply it in the moment of need.

Today, that velocity is being destroyed by knowledge fragmentation:

- People can’t find what they need fast enough (or trust what they find). A Gartner survey found **47% of digital workers struggle to find the information needed** to do their jobs. ([Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2023-05-10-gartner-survey-reveals-47-percent-of-digital-workers-struggle-to-find-the-information-needed-to-effectively-perform-their-jobs?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- Even when info exists, “productive hours” vanish into searching, rework, and waiting on answers. APQC reported the average knowledge worker gets **~30 productive hours out of a 40-hour week**. ([APQC](https://www.apqc.org/about-apqc/news-press-release/apqc-survey-finds-one-quarter-knowledge-workers-time-lost-due?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

So the real strategy isn’t “implement AI.”

It’s: **build an AI-native knowledge system that turns your company’s content into usable, queryable, decision-grade context—at scale.**

## People Transformation

One of the biggest opportunities for organizations today, is simply how fast they transform the people within their organization to increasingly leverage AI to automate the work they can, and pivot in how they approach their work, and the decisions they make, in order to steer their roles, their teams, their departments, and the organization as a whole in the direction of highest value.

## Leaders and HR

The people responsible for our people. And that’s leaders and HR. These two segments of the organization represent the most reliable and sure pathway to long-term organizational advantage.

Your job isn’t HR. It’s organizational capability.

Talent strategy now equals knowledge strategy.

As an HR leader, you’re the only executive function that legitimately owns the system of how people learn, ramp, and stay aligned as the business changes.
